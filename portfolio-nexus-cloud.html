The Challenge

The client — a high-growth fintech serving 400,000 active users — was running a monolithic application on a single Lagos VPS. As transaction volumes tripled in 12 months, the platform suffered weekly outages and peak-hour slowdowns that were directly eroding customer trust and revenue.

They needed to migrate to the cloud, adopt a microservices architecture, and prepare for 10M users — without a single minute of planned downtime during the cutover.

architecture

Our Approach

  • Discovery (Weeks 1–2): Full audit of the existing stack, traffic patterns, and database schemas. Identified 12 services ready for extraction.
  • Parallel build (Weeks 3–12): Containerised each service with Docker, deployed on EKS across Lagos, London, and Dublin AWS regions. Set up PostgreSQL RDS with automated failover.
  • Blue-green cutover (Week 16): Live traffic shifted gradually from legacy to cloud via AWS Route 53 weighted routing — 5% → 25% → 100% over 4 hours with real-time monitoring in Datadog.
  • Optimise (Weeks 17–18): CloudFront CDN tuned, auto-scaling policies configured, runbooks and alerting handed to the client engineering team.
